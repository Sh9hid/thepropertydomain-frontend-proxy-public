## SCRIPT 1: Cold Call — Developer Target Lead

**Duration:** 3-5 minutes
**Goal:** Get permission to send the prospectus and book a 15-min follow-up

### Opening (30 seconds)

> "Hi [Name], it's Shahid calling from Laing+Simmons Oakville. I'm reaching out because your property at [address] came up in our spatial data as fitting a very specific profile that institutional buyers are actively pursuing in [suburb]. Do you have a quick minute?"

**If "no":**
> "No worries at all. Can I send you a one-page summary by text? It takes 30 seconds to read and it shows what developers are actually paying for land in your corridor. [Get mobile/email]"

**If "yes" / curious:**

### Value Hook (60 seconds)

> "So what we've identified is that [suburb] has had [X] development applications lodged in the last 6 months within 500 metres of your property. That's creating a gap between what residential buyers would pay and what developers will pay. On a block like yours — [size]sqm — that gap can be significant.

> What we do is produce a 7-page institutional-grade yield prospectus — not a standard appraisal — that shows the exact developer yield calculation for your land based on FSR, zoning, and live DA data. It's the same analysis institutional buyers commission before they make offers."

### Soft Close (30 seconds)

> "I'd like to send you that report — completely free, no obligation. You can sit on it, share it with your partner, use it as a benchmark. Would email or text work better for you?"

**If they ask about selling:**
> "The report speaks for itself. Once you've reviewed it, if you want to explore options — whether that's now or in two years — I'm here. No pressure at all."

### Book the Follow-Up

> "I'll have the report to you by [day]. Would [2 days later] work for a 10-minute call so I can walk you through the numbers? It'll make a lot more sense with a quick explanation."

## SCRIPT 2: Cold Call — Probate / Executor Lead

**Duration:** 3-4 minutes
**Goal:** Offer the prospectus as a tool that protects the executor's position

### Opening (30 seconds)

> "Hi [Name], it's Shahid from Laing+Simmons Oakville. I'm calling regarding the property at [address]. I understand you may be managing the estate, and I wanted to offer something that might be useful — a confidential property intelligence report. Is this a good time?"

**Critical:** Be respectful. If they sound distressed, offer to call back. Never push.

### Value Hook (45 seconds)

> "What I can provide is a detailed institutional-grade valuation report for the property. It's different from a standard appraisal because it includes developer yield analysis and off-market demand data. The reason that matters for an estate is that it gives you — as executor — defensible evidence of fair market value. Solicitors appreciate it because it strengthens the file."

### Soft Close (30 seconds)

> "I can prepare this at no cost. If the estate decides to explore a sale down the track, we specialise in discreet, off-market disposals — no signage, no open homes. But the report is yours regardless. Shall I send it to you, or would you prefer I send it directly to the estate solicitor as well?"

### If They Say "The Solicitor Handles Everything"

> "Completely understand. Would you be comfortable sharing their details? I can send them the report directly — it often helps their process. And if they have any questions about the methodology, I'm happy to walk them through it."

## SCRIPT 4: Warm Follow-Up (Post-Prospectus Send)

**Duration:** 5-10 minutes
**Goal:** Walk through the report, answer questions, book a face-to-face

### Opening

> "Hi [Name], it's Shahid. I sent through the property intelligence report for [address] on [day]. Did you get a chance to have a look?"

**If they haven't read it:**
> "No problem — I know it's detailed. The key number is on page 3: the developer yield gap. Your property's residential estimate is around $[X], but the institutional yield calculation comes in at $[Y]. That's a gap of $[Z]. Want me to walk you through how we get to that number?"

**If they've read it:**
> "Great. What stood out to you? [Listen first — don't pitch. Their questions tell you where they are in the decision.]"

### Walk-Through Points

1. **Page 2-3: The yield gap** — "This is the difference between what a family buyer would pay and what a developer would pay, based on the FSR and approved land use."
2. **Page 4: Comparable transactions** — "These are real off-market developer acquisitions in your corridor. Not residential sales — developer deals."
3. **Page 5: Zoning and DA activity** — "This shows why the timing matters. [X] DAs lodged means the area is actively being acquired."

### Close

> "Based on what you've seen, would it be useful to sit down for 15 minutes — over coffee if you prefer — to talk through what your options look like? No commitment, just a conversation with the data in front of us."

## UNIVERSAL RULES FOR ALL CALLS

1. **Never say "free appraisal"** — say "institutional-grade property intelligence report"
2. **Never say "just listed" or "just sold"** — say "off-market developer acquisition"
3. **Never pressure** — the prospectus does the work; you're the delivery mechanism
4. **Always get a next step** — send report, book callback, get email, get mobile
5. **Always confirm identity by channel** — Shahid on calls/SMS, Nitin Puri on email
6. **Log the outcome immediately** — the system needs the data to learn
7. **If they say no, ask for the one-pager send** — worst case you're in their file drawer
