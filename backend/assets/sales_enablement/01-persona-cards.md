## PERSONA 1: The Developer Target

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Role** | Homeowner sitting on 600sqm+ land in a rezoned or R3/R4 corridor |
| **Typical profile** | 55-75, owned 15+ years, empty nesters, may not follow planning changes |
| **Goals** | Maximise exit value; retire comfortably; avoid the stress of a drawn-out campaign |
| **Daily frustrations** | Generic letterbox drops from agents who quote residential comps; no idea developers are circling their street; fear of selling too cheap |
| **Top objections** | "We aren't looking to sell." / "We already get appraisals." / "We'll wait for the market to improve." |
| **Evaluation criteria** | Data quality of the prospectus; evidence of developer demand; discretion; fee transparency |
| **Buying process** | Usually a couple — both must agree. One partner is often the gatekeeper. Decision takes 2-8 weeks after receiving the report. |
| **Role in decision** | Economic buyer AND end user — they own the asset and make the call |
| **Messaging angle** | "Your property fits a specific high-yield profile that developers are actively pursuing. I have the data to prove it." |
| **Lead with** | The 7-page yield prospectus showing developer vs. residential gap |
| **Avoid** | Pressure tactics, "just listed" language, generic market updates |

**Trigger signals:** DA lodged on adjacent lot, R3/R4 rezoning within 500m, land size 600sqm+, corner block, dual-street frontage, probate filing.

## PERSONA 2: The Executor

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Role** | Executor of a deceased estate, often an adult child or solicitor |
| **Typical profile** | 40-60, emotionally drained, juggling probate court timelines, may not live locally |
| **Goals** | Liquidate the asset efficiently, fairly, and discreetly; satisfy beneficiaries; minimise legal exposure |
| **Daily frustrations** | Overwhelmed by paperwork; agents who don't understand probate timelines; pressure from co-beneficiaries; fear of underselling and being challenged |
| **Top objections** | "We need to get probate granted first." / "The solicitor is handling everything." / "We're not ready to decide." |
| **Evaluation criteria** | Speed to sale; discretion (no open homes, no signage); evidence-based pricing they can defend to beneficiaries; experience with estate sales |
| **Buying process** | Executor has legal authority but often needs to consult co-beneficiaries or the estate solicitor. Timeline is court-driven (3-6 months post-grant). |
| **Role in decision** | Economic buyer with fiduciary obligation — must demonstrate fair market process |
| **Messaging angle** | "I specialise in discreet estate disposals with institutional-grade valuations that protect you as executor." |
| **Lead with** | Speed, discretion, and defensible valuation data (protects them legally) |
| **Avoid** | Anything that feels like ambulance chasing; never cold-call within 2 weeks of a death notice |

**Trigger signals:** Supreme Court probate filing, property in deceased estate with no recent sale history, solicitor enquiry.

## PERSONA 3: The Mortgagee (Mortgage Cliff)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Role** | Homeowner whose fixed-rate mortgage is expiring in the next 6 months |
| **Typical profile** | 35-55, dual income, locked in at 2-3% during COVID, facing a jump to 6%+ |
| **Goals** | Reduce monthly repayments; understand their equity position; keep the house if possible, sell strategically if not |
| **Daily frustrations** | Anxiety about the rate reset; confusion about refinancing options; don't know who to trust; banks are unhelpful |
| **Top objections** | "Our broker is handling it." / "We're just going to ride it out." / "We don't want to sell." |
| **Evaluation criteria** | Whether the refinance saves them real money; clarity of the equity position; no-pressure approach; dual service (sell OR refinance) |
| **Buying process** | Usually a couple. The one who manages finances makes the initial call. Decision within 2-4 weeks — urgency increases as the reset date approaches. |
| **Role in decision** | Economic buyer — directly impacted by the financial outcome |
| **Messaging angle** | "Your fixed rate expires in [X months]. I can run a free equity and refinance health check — no obligation. If selling makes more sense, I'll show you the numbers for that too." |
| **Lead with** | Proactive refinance health check through Ownit1st; equity position analysis |
| **Avoid** | Fear-mongering about rates; never imply they'll lose the house |

**Trigger signals:** Fixed-rate expiry data from mortgage intelligence, property with equity above LVR threshold, refinance enquiry.

## PERSONA 4: The Blocker (Spouse / Co-owner / Solicitor)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Role** | The person who didn't take the call but influences the decision |
| **Typical profile** | Varies — could be a protective spouse, a cautious co-beneficiary, or an estate solicitor |
| **Their concern** | "Is this person legitimate?" / "Are we being pressured?" / "Can we trust the data?" |
| **How to neutralise** | Send the prospectus directly (it sells for you); offer a no-obligation second meeting; never go around them — always include them |
| **Messaging angle** | "I completely understand you want to review this together. I'll send the full report so you can both look at the data at your own pace." |

## PERSONA 5: The Champion (Referral Source)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Role** | Solicitor, financial planner, accountant, or neighbour who refers a lead |
| **Typical profile** | Professional with a client portfolio; values reciprocity and discretion |
