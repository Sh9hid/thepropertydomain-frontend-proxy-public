## 1. How We Sell

### The Model

We don't sell real estate services. We sell **data-backed certainty** in an uncertain market.

The prospectus is the product. The sale is the outcome. The operator's job is to:
1. Identify the signal (the system does this)
2. Deliver the intelligence (the prospectus)
3. Be available when they're ready to act

### The Funnel

```
Signal detected (DA, rezoning, probate, mortgage cliff)
        |
   Cold outreach (call, SMS, door knock)
        |
   Permission to send prospectus
        |
   Prospectus delivered
        |
   Follow-up call (walk through the data)
        |
   Face-to-face meeting (coffee / site visit)
        |
   Proposal sent
        |
   Agency agreement signed
        |
   Off-market campaign executed
        |
   Sale / Refinance completed
```

### Conversion Benchmarks

| Stage | Target Rate |
|-------|------------|
| Cold call → permission to send | 25-30% |
| Prospectus sent → follow-up completed | 60% |
| Follow-up → face-to-face meeting | 30% |
| Meeting → proposal sent | 50% |
| Proposal → signed | 40% |

## 2. Qualification Framework

Use **BANT-D** (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline, Data-fit):

| Criterion | What to Assess | Qualified If |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Budget** | Can they actually sell? Any debt, legal, or family constraints? | No insurmountable blockers to a sale or refinance |
| **Authority** | Are you talking to the decision-maker or a gatekeeper? | Talking to the owner, executor, or primary mortgage holder |
| **Need** | Is there a genuine trigger? | DA nearby, rate expiring, estate to settle, considering downsizing |
| **Timeline** | When might they act? | Within 12 months. If "never," nurture but don't chase |
| **Data-fit** | Does the property actually have a developer premium? | Land 500sqm+, zoned R3+, or in an active DA corridor |

### Disqualify Fast

Don't waste time on:
- Apartments or strata (no developer value)
- Properties under 400sqm with no rezoning potential
- People who are hostile or on the Do Not Call register
- Properties with caveats, disputes, or active litigation (unless estate)

## 3. Discovery Questions by Persona

### Developer Target

1. "How long have you owned the property?"
2. "Have you noticed much construction activity on your street lately?"
3. "Has anyone else approached you about the development potential?"
4. "Are you and your partner both on the title?"
5. "Have you ever had a developer approach you directly?"
6. "What would need to be true for you to consider selling in the next 12 months?"
7. "If I could show you that your land is worth significantly more to a developer than to a residential buyer, would that change anything?"

### Executor

1. "I understand you're managing the estate. How are you finding the process?"
2. "Has the grant of probate been issued, or is that still in progress?"
3. "Are there multiple beneficiaries, or are you the sole executor?"
4. "Is there a timeline from the solicitor for when the property should be dealt with?"
5. "Is discretion important for the family?"
6. "Have you had the property valued yet? I can provide an institutional-grade report at no cost."

### Mortgagee

1. "Do you know when your fixed rate expires?"
2. "Have you looked at what the revert rate will be?"
3. "Have you explored refinancing options yet?"
4. "Are you currently working with a broker?"
5. "If the refinance numbers don't work, would you consider selling and using the equity to reset?"
6. "Would a free 10-minute health check be useful — just to see where you stand?"

## 4. The Sales Process (Stage by Stage)

### Stage 1: Signal Detection (Automated)

The system identifies leads via:
- NSW DA API (development applications within target suburbs)
- Supreme Court probate filings
- Domain/REA withdrawn listings (frustrated vendors)
- Mortgage cliff data (fixed-rate expiry)
- RP Data / CoreLogic zoning changes
- Door-knock campaigns (manual)

**Operator action:** Review the daily call list. The system scores and prioritises.

### Stage 2: Cold Outreach

**Channel priority:**
1. Phone call (highest conversion)
2. SMS (for missed calls or after hours)
3. Door knock (for high-value targets in cluster areas)
4. Email (for follow-up or formal contact)

**Identity rules:**
- Calls/SMS: Shahid
- Email/formal: Nitin Puri, oakville@lsre.com.au
- Mortgage calls: Shahid, Ownit1st branding

**Goal:** Get permission to send the prospectus.

### Stage 3: Prospectus Delivery

- Send within 24 hours of permission
- Email from oakville@lsre.com.au
- Include a brief cover note (see email templates below)
- SMS confirmation: "Hi [Name], Shahid here. Just sent the report to your email — let me know if it doesn't come through."

### Stage 4: Follow-Up Call

- Call 2-3 days after sending
- Walk through the key numbers (yield gap, DA activity)
- Listen more than you talk — their questions reveal their position
- Goal: book a face-to-face or get a clear timeline

### Stage 5: Face-to-Face Meeting

- Coffee meeting or site visit (their choice)
- Bring a printed copy of the prospectus
- Bring the proposal template (customised)
- Don't hard-close — present the options, let the data do the work

### Stage 6: Proposal & Close

- Send the customised proposal within 24 hours of the meeting
- Follow up by phone 3-5 days later
- If they need more time, set a check-in date and respect it
- Never chase — signal confidence

| They Do | We Do |
|---------|-------|
| Residential comparables | Developer yield analysis |
| Sign out front + Domain listing | Confidential off-market approach |
| Generic vendor-paid advertising | Targeted information memorandum to developer network |
| "We think it's worth about $X" | "Here is the institutional data — the yield says $Y" |

**Win line:** "They sell houses. We match land to the right institutional buyer using data they don't have access to."

### vs. Automated Valuation Models (Domain/REA Estimates)

| AVM Says | We Say |
|----------|--------|
| Based on residential sales within 1km | Based on FSR, zoning, and developer yield |
| Backward-looking (recent sales) | Forward-looking (DA activity, zoning shifts) |
| Same number for every property type | Different calculation for development sites |

**Win line:** "An AVM can't tell you what a developer will pay. We can."

### vs. "We Already Have a Broker" (Mortgage)

**Response:** "That's great. We're not replacing them — we're offering a free second opinion. If your broker's rate is the best available, you'll know for sure. If it's not, you'll have options."

| Metric | Target | Why |

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Listings signed | 2-3/month | Revenue driver |
| Avg days: signal → prospectus sent | < 3 days | Speed matters |
| Avg days: prospectus → meeting | < 10 days | Momentum indicator |
| Prospectus → signed conversion | 15-20% | Playbook effectiveness |
| Avg developer premium achieved | 25%+ above residential | Value proposition proof |

### Review Quarterly

- Which signal types convert best? (DA, probate, mortgage, withdrawn)
- Which suburbs have the highest yield gaps?
- Which call scripts get the most prospectus permissions?
- Which objection responses work and which need updating?
- Are the suburb yield benchmarks still accurate?
